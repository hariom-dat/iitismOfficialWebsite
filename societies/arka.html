ARKA - Astronomy Club

About

Astronomy Club was founded in 5th December 2017 by Shashank Kumar (2018 batch), Sucharita Kommu (2018 batch), Karan Pal (2018 batch), Ishan Srivastava (2018 batch), Ramendra Ranjan (2018 batch), Mayank Maurya (2019 batch).

Vision

Our vision for the club can culminate into two things:

1. To bring together like-minded people and provide them with a platform to discuss the subject as a hobby, and indulge in intriguing activities like telescopic observations.

2. To provide interested students with basic knowledge about what happens up above and familiarize the members with Astronomy by conducting various events. All are welcome to be a part of this club. No prior knowledge in astronomy is required. An interest and passion for the field is what we seek.

Journey

A starry night sky is a wonder. Ever looked up to the night sky and wondered about the stars, space and all other heavenly bodies?

Similarly, Having harbored the dreams of studying about space, stars and galaxies since childhood, the founders of the club decided to bring together a platform to share ideas, learn and evolve as amateur astronomers. Though the concept of the club pre-existed in IIT(ISM), it was in the year 2017 that the efforts took shape, and the Astronomy club began with induction and opening session on 5th December 2017.

Since then we have had various sessions, covering a wide range of activities like Sky-watching, Observatory sessions, Video lectures, Guest talks, special events, telescope making and other allied activities.
We invited “Team Indus”, India’s first Space Startup to deliver a guest talk at Concetto-’17(annual tech fest). During Super Blue Blood Moon on 31st Jan, our telescopic observatory session witnessed huge crowd from B.Tech, M.Tech scholars and professors alike. This academic year started with PRIMUS-18 (Guest talks’ event) in which the invitees were Dr. Xavier James of VSSC-ISRO, Prof. Ranjan Gupta of IUCAA Pune, Prof. Bijay Sharma of NIT Patna and for the Concetto-18 we invited India’s youngest astronomer ‘Aryan Mishra’. Apart from it, we oversaw the first level of National Student Space Challenge-’18 (NSSC) in our college. Our online quiz event ‘quARKA’ was conducted during World Space Week in October.

Club Routine

    The monthly club routine is divided as follows into four weeks:

  • Models / Project Presentations
  • Telescopic Sky Observational Sessions
  • Presentations on various topics of Astronomy and Physics
  • Guest Talks

    • Apart from these, we conduct observational sessions, documentary screenings and competitions(‘quARKA’ online quiz) on special astronomical events.
