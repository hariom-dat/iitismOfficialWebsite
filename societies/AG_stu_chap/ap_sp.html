AIM


AAPG Students chapter IIT(ISM) Dhanbad was established in the year 2009 in the Department of Applied Geology.
It aims at developing a strong platform for interaction and exchange of knowledge between industry and academia through many events like keynote addresses, workshops, competitive events and interactive sessions in which industry professionals are directly involved with the students and Faculty Advisor. Every year we organize AAPG annual meet which brings together the students and the alumni under one roof and encourage interactive dialogue. They play a very important role in strengthening the bond with alumni. The aim of these meets is to connect the alumni and the students at a very personal level and ensure long term interaction and strong bonding thus proving to be extremely beneficial to both the students as well as the alumni.



Vision

AAPG has the following two visions:

  • Advancing the science of petroleum geology.
  • Inspiring the petroleum-geologist of today and tomorrow, AAPG's long-standing tradition of excellence in education, professional development, and engagement cultivates a unique community platform that encourages collaboration and thought leadership for the advancement of petroleum geology around the world.



Chapter Benefits

Petroleum industry giant like Cairn India, ONGC, OIL India, IOCL etc consider AAPG IIT(ISM), Dhanbad Student Chapter as a good platform for reaching out to students with the challenges faced by industry and to introduce them with the Industry culture at a student level. They even share their life and professional experiences with students and give them insights as to how to expand the chapter. The alumni maintain their links with the Chapter.


The chapter helps in enhancing the:

  • The team working capabilities
  • It helps in gaining practical knowledge about about how the industry works.
  • It helps in improving the interpersonal skills.
  • Student Travel Awards (of $800) for student participants at SIAM conferences with free registration.

  • A geotechnical workshop was organized in Mumbai, India in December 2017 by AAPG for the people from geosciences to gather and showcase advancements in geotechnical fields by the means of oral or static poster presentations. The program was held under the esteemed supervision of then AAPG president Peter Grant, and was managed by Adrienne Pereira, AAPG Asia Pacific Region Programs Manager. Not only the working group from oil and gas industries of the country, but also the students from IIT(ISM), Dhanbad took active part in showcasing their research works and volunteering the program. The whole event was held in Ramada, Powai and about 100 registered participants and volunteers were present.
  • Oil and gas industries like Oil and Natural Gas Corporation (ONGC), Oil India Limited (OIL), Cairn India Limited, Reliance Industries and also foreign industries like Baker Huges were also represented by delegates. In addition to the delegates from India, GTW welcomed delegates from Australia, Egypt, New Zealand, Spain, UK and the USA.
  • The students from IIT(ISM) Dhanbad who were present as volunteers were Pratibha Srivastava, (Integrated MSc. Tech, Applied Geology), Apourv Pandey(Integrated MSc Tech, Applied Geology), Jenifer Alam (Research Scholar, Applied Geophysics), Triveni Gogoi (Research Scholar, Applied Geophysics).
  • Pratibha Srivastava presented a static poster on “A preliminary Analysis of Edge-Effects in True-Triaxially Deformed Darley Dale Sandstone Samples”. Apourv Pandey delivered an oral paper presentation on “Effects of Different Map Interpolation Techniques On The TWT Surface Map”.
  • The AAPG Student Chapter of IIT(ISM) Dhanbad conducted a two-day guest lecture on Petroleum Geology and Hydrocarbon Exploration by Padmashree Dr. Rabi Bastia, who is an Indian Geoscientist and the Global Head of Exploration at OilMaxx Energy, Mumbai. The lecture consisted of 4 sessions in the Morning and Evening on 3rd and 4th March, 2017 in the Department of Applied Geology. The lectures covered topics such as Well Logging, Seismic Interpretation, Reservoir Estimation and understanding the Petroleum System. The first session in the morning of 3rd March, 2017 was mainly focussed on understanding the basics of well logging.
  • The lecture was attended by the students of Department of Applied Geology, Applied Geophysics and Petroleum Engineering. Thus, the lecture gave a practical experience on working with well log data, seismic data and the students-built confidence towards solving a practical industrial problem in hydrocarbon exploration.
